WHAT WE OBSERVED

1. Independent Silicon Paths Are Real
The GPU and Neural Engine are genuinely separate compute blocks. Running a model on one has near-zero impact on the other's throughput. The only shared resource is the unified memory bus.
2. Dual-Model Inference Works on Consumer Hardware
A 9B model on GPU + a 1B model on ANE coexist with a combined footprint of ~6.6 GB — well within 16 GB. Both produce responses simultaneously.
3. ANE Power Efficiency Enables Always-On Workloads
The ANE runs a 1B model at 53.8 tok/s while consuming ~2W. The GPU runs a 9B model at 19.9 tok/s at ~15-20W. The raw tok/s difference reflects model size, not hardware speed — but the power efficiency gap is real: the ANE delivers useful inference at a fraction of the energy cost, enabling always-on background workloads that would drain battery on the GPU.
4. The Bottleneck Is Model Loading, Not Inference
ANE decode is 53.8 tok/s (1.86s for 100 tokens), but subprocess model loading adds ~5s overhead. A persistent ANEMLL server would cut total ANE time from 7.6s to ~2.7s.
